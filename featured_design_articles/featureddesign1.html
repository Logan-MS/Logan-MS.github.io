Aquarium Prototype Article


  When creating the aquarium prototype design, I specifically wanted it to mirror the environment of an aquarium which is often a mixture of dark shadows and the lightness of the fish. The homepage design was created to make a mixture of interesting imagery that would draw a visitor into the site as well as provide some basic information giving a quick summary if a user doesn't have time for the more detailed information.


  I designed the exhibits page to be eyecatching and focus on popular exhibits of an aquarium to draw the attention of visitors. Additionally, I gave descriptive titles for each sub-article and provided a summary of each exhibit offered. The grid was utilized to break up the sections in an aesthetically pleasing manner so a reader would not feel overwhelmed by a wall of text. This also offers better readability.


  My main goal when designing the visit us page was to make the information, the text, the main area of focus. Most visitors would be primarily interested in learning about fees, tours/attractions, hours, and the location. However, I did have one centerfold image to add some visual diversity to the page.


I wanted the gift shop to be unique and simple to navigate, so I opted for utilizing another grid this time making the images different sizes so each featured product got attention and was easy to find.


The last section of the site prototype was the footer. It had basic contact data, clear social media icon links, and copyright. It was created to feel breathable by giving it ample space which allowed the content to not feel overwhelming.
